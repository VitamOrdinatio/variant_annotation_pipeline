## Overview

This artifact summarizes the composition of **Tier 2 (moderate-priority) candidate variants** by variant origin.

---

## Candidate Composition (Tier 2)

| Variant Origin | Count |
|----------------|--------|
| noncoding      | 112,242 |
| coding         | 1,121   |

---

## Interpretation

- The vast majority of candidate variants (~99%) are **noncoding**  
- A small subset (~1%) are **coding variants**  

### Key Insight

> Candidate prioritization is dominated by noncoding variation, reflecting the underlying structure of the genome.

---

## Biological Context

- Noncoding regions represent >99% of the genome  
- Most genomic variation occurs outside coding regions  
- Coding variants, while fewer, are more directly interpretable  

---

## System Implications

- Coding candidates provide **higher-confidence interpretability**  
- Noncoding candidates require:
  - regulatory annotation  
  - transcriptomic integration (RSP)  
  - functional validation  

---

## Important Considerations

- HG002 is a healthy benchmark genome  
- candidate variants do not imply disease  
- prioritization reflects **structure, not diagnosis**  

---

## Bottom Line

> Stage 11 candidate composition reflects genome architecture: noncoding variants dominate quantity, while coding variants provide interpretable signal.
