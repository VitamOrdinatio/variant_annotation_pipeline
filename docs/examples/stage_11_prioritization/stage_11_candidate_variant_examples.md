## Selection Criteria

Examples were selected to illustrate:

- rare coding variants (missense / LOF)
- variants with clinical annotations (VUS, benign)
- variants spanning different frequency categories

These examples are representative, not exhaustive.

## Why These Examples Matter

These examples illustrate how Stage 11:

- elevates rare, interpretable coding variants  
- deprioritizes common or low-impact variants  
- integrates coding and noncoding signals into a unified framework  

They demonstrate the types of variants that survive prioritization,
not definitive disease-associated findings.
