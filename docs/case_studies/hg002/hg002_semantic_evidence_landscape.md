# WGS Semantic Topology

HG002 whole-genome sequencing (WGS) produced a substantially expanded noncoding semantic evidence surface compared to prior epilepsy-focused whole-exome sequencing (WES) case studies.

This distinction is expected for WGS-scale analysis.

Whereas WES primarily concentrates evidence density within protein-coding regions, WGS exposes substantially larger:

* intronic surfaces,
* intergenic regions,
* transcript-associated noncoding regions,
* flanking regulatory contexts,
* broader low-interpretability evidence classes.

The HG002 semantic branching architecture demonstrated that VAP preserves these evidence classes explicitly rather than suppressing them during downstream organization.

The resulting topology showed:

* large noncoding evidence expansion,
* preserved coding/noncoding separation,
* rarity-aware semantic routing,
* interpretability-aware decomposition,
* stable downstream prioritization behavior.

Importantly, noncoding expansion does not imply clinical relevance.

Rather, it reflects the expected increase in observable semantic evidence space produced by whole-genome sequencing.

# Coding vs Noncoding Organizational Behavior

The HG002 semantic evidence architecture demonstrated markedly different organizational behavior between coding and noncoding evidence classes.

Coding evidence remained comparatively constrained and interpretation-oriented, with enrichment for:

* protein-altering consequences,
* splice-associated events,
* ClinVar-linked evidence,
* functional consequence stratification,
* prioritization-ready routing.

Noncoding evidence instead produced broad observational evidence surfaces dominated by:

* intronic variants,
* intergenic contexts,
* transcript-associated noncoding regions,
* low-support/common evidence classes,
* high-volume uncertain-significance annotations.

This distinction is expected for WGS-scale analysis.

VAP preserves these evidence classes as semantically distinct organizational layers rather than forcing homogenization into a single interpretive framework.

This separation is scientifically important because semantic evidence organization is not equivalent to diagnosis, causality, pathogenicity, or validation status.

Instead, VAP preserves evidence topology while enabling downstream systems and analysts to apply context-specific interpretation strategies.

# Scientific Caveats

Several important scientific constraints apply to the HG002 benchmarking and semantic evidence landscape analyses.

First, `hap.py` benchmarking validates upstream substrate concordance against GIAB truth resources inside high-confidence benchmark regions. Benchmarking does not validate downstream biological interpretation or clinical interpretation.

Second, semantic evidence organization does not imply diagnosis, pathogenicity, or validation status.

Third, noncoding evidence classes primarily represent observational semantic surfaces rather than direct clinical assertions.

Fourth, deterministic prioritization layers preserve organizational structure rather than establishing biological truth.

Finally, HG002 represents a benchmarking-oriented reference genome substrate rather than a disease-focused clinical case study.

These caveats are important for maintaining clear separation between engineering validation, semantic evidence organization, and downstream biological interpretation.
